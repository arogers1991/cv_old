Axl Floyd Rogers

Technician at the Warkworth Radio Astronomical Observatory

PhD (Astronomy and Physics) candidate at the Auckland University of Technology (AUT).

Using state-of-the-art, high-fidelity polarimetric calibration techniques, we aim to improve pulsar signal processing and demonstrate reduced noise in pulsar TOAs. We propose to apply this to a list of all 26 millisecond pulsars that are regularly observed by the PPTA collaboration. This will create a dataset of the best pulsar TOAs which we will then be able to analyse using red noise modelling tools. Our goal is to place the most stringest upper limits on the stochastic gravitational wave background.
